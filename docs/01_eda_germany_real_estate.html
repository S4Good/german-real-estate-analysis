Key Findings (Summary)

  • The dataset contains 10,552 listings across Germany.
  • Many columns have missing values, especially energy-related data.
  • Property prices are strongly right-skewed (few very expensive listings), so I used a log scale to visualize the distribution.
  • After removing extreme outliers, the cleaned dataset contains 10,055 listings.
  • Median price varies strongly by location:
    • Top expensive cities include premium districts (e.g., Berlin districts and high-demand Bavarian regions).
  • For better comparison, I calculated Price per m (/m).
    • After removing unrealistic /m outliers, 9,685 listings remained.
    • Hamburg and Berlin show the highest median /m among the top states.
